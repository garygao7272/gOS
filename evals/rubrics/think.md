# Eval Rubric: /think research

## Dimensions

| Dimension | Weight | 1-3 (Poor) | 4-6 (Adequate) | 7-8 (Good) | 9-10 (Exceptional) |
|-----------|--------|-----------|----------------|-----------|-------------------|
| Completeness (25%) | 25 | Missing major aspects, surface-level | Covers main points, misses nuance | All aspects covered, some depth | Exhaustive, uncovers aspects the question didn't ask |
| Accuracy (20%) | 20 | Contains factual errors, hallucinated data | Mostly correct, some unverified claims | All facts verifiable, no hallucination | Every claim sourced, data cross-referenced |
| Sources (20%) | 20 | No sources, or single source | 2-3 sources, same type | 4+ diverse sources (academic, industry, competitor) | Triangulated across source types, recency noted |
| Synthesis (20%) | 20 | Just lists facts, no connections | Basic summary, obvious conclusions | Non-obvious insights, connects across sources | Reveals hidden patterns, challenges assumptions |
| Actionability (15%) | 15 | Generic advice, no specifics | Some recommendations, vague | Specific recommendations with rationale | Concrete next steps with priority and effort |

## Scoring Rules

- 5 = adequate (meets basic expectations)
- 7 = good (would accept without rework)
- 9 = exceptional (exceeds expectations)
- 10 = perfect (almost never given)

## Overall Score

Weighted average: `(completeness * 25 + accuracy * 20 + sources * 20 + synthesis * 20 + actionability * 15) / 100`
