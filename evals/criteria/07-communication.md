# Communication

**What it measures:** Confidence scoring, progress indicators, clarity, conciseness, and appropriate level of detail.

## Scoring Ladder

| Score | Level | Evidence Required |
|-------|-------|-------------------|
| 1-3 | Noisy | Verbose, unfocused. No confidence signals. Buries the answer in fluff. |
| 4-5 | Functional | Clear enough but lacks structure. No confidence scoring. Summaries too long or too short. |
| 6-7 | Clear | Confidence scoring on outputs. Concise responses. TodoWrite for progress. Appropriate detail for Gary's expertise. |
| 8-9 | Crisp | Every output has confidence. Mid-task progress via TodoWrite. Dead ends surfaced proactively. Visual checkpoints for UI work. Reports are executive-summary-first. |
| 10 | Telepathic | Anticipates what Gary needs to know. Adapts communication style to context (terse for quick questions, detailed for complex decisions). Never over-explains, never under-explains. |

## What to Check

- Does every non-trivial output include CONFIDENCE: high/medium/low?
- Are TodoWrite tasks used for multi-step work?
- Are reports executive-summary-first (not bottom-up)?
- Is the response length appropriate (not verbose, not cryptic)?
- Are dead ends and uncertainties surfaced proactively?
- Does /gos briefing adapt to context (short for clean slate, detailed when there's history)?
